About the Codex

What this is, why it exists, and who made it.

What is the Peasant's Book?

The Peasant's Book is a medieval reference codex built for people who want real answers without wading through academic language or endless footnotes. Think of it as Wikipedia, but stripped down, focused, and designed for the Middle Ages only.

Every entry covers exactly what you need to know. No filler, no tangents. Just the essential history, clearly told.

Why "Peasant's Book"?

In medieval Europe, knowledge was gatekept. Books were written in Latin, owned by the Church, and completely inaccessible to ordinary people. The Peasant's Book is named in defiance of that, history written for everyone, not just scholars.

Sources & Accuracy

Entries are written based on established historical scholarship. Where historians disagree, we say so. Always cross-reference with academic sources for serious research.

The Project

The Peasant's Book is an independent effort to make medieval history accessible to everyone. Built with care, designed with intention, no frameworks, no templates, just craftsmanship.
